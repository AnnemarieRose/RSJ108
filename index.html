Photojournalism uses photography to report current events and issues. This career is highly competitive career and can take years of hard work to be successful. However, getting to that goal is not out of reach. To be a photojournalist you need to have a passion for people, their stories, and a vision for capturing them with photography.

  • Things You'll Need

  • Camera
  • Passion
  • Portfolio
  • Patience
  • Photoshop
  • Adobe Illustrator

  1. Get Your Degree.

    Education is often required to get good photojournalism position; a degree also strengthens your resume. Having an education can help you grow as a photojournalist, help you learn basic skills, and teaches you how to use constructive criticism and use it for good. While you are studying photojournalism build your portfolio. .

  2. Get to Know Other Photojournalists.

    During your education always make connections with photojournalists that you meet. Get involved with your university or local newspaper, offer to take photos for newsletters or websites. After you have graduated stay in contact to those you have made connections within the industry. Reach out to photojournalists that you find inspiring and do your part to research and find your own work..

    .

  3. Look into getting an internship.

    Internships are a great way to gain experience as well as build your resume. It is more likely that you will get a job within the field if you have had previous internship experiece. If you are working on your degree, ask your professors for any recommendations about possible internships and where to apply. Just keep in mind that internships can be very competitive, so keeping an updated portfolio would be a good idea. .

  4. Freelance Business.

    One option to gain skill and build your portfolio is to be a freelancer. Freelancing is becoming more common especially with the rise of social media. Freelancing teaches you how to market yourself and make connections. .

  5. Get a Career With a Magazine or Newspaper Company.
